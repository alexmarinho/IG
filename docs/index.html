    The race — 6 searches, one instance ·

    Real implementations of the arena algorithms running right now, in your browser, on the same instance — all paying in the same currency: cost-function evaluations (the fair meter; no VBA vs C++). Each search grows a tree: the trunk is the accepted path, twigs and halos are discarded attempts (the explored area), orange stretches are deliberate uphill moves, dashed arcs are diversification jumps, and the ring marks the best leaf found. The shape of the tree is the personality of the algorithm.

    The solution space — every leaf is a possible schedule

    Before the race we sample ~400 real schedules for this instance and arrange them as a tree: branches group schedules that share ancestry (similar orderings and rejections), height is quality — cheaper is higher — and the gold leaf is the best sample. Each search is a firefly: watch it wander the canopy (its live working solution) while its pin marks the best it has locked in. Crossing into the golden band means it beat every sampled possibility — from there on it is chasing the true optimum leaf.

    The Showdown — the thesis vs. the literature

    Thevenin, Zufferey & Widmer (2015) attacked this same problem with 6 heuristics in C++ (i7 2.93 GHz), plus a MILP given 1 h per instance. The thesis IG — written in Excel VBA (i7 2.3 GHz) — entered that arena: 44 MaScLib instances, 5 runs each, stopping at n×30 s.     A faithful miniature of the algorithm from alexmarinho/IG: greedy initial construction (EDD order, insert only if it beats rejecting), then the destroy-reinsert loop restarting from the best solution. Total cost = setup + tardiness penalties + rejection. Setups carry time and cost, dependent on the previous job’s family — and a setup may start before the job’s release. Arena data: Tables 4.1–4.2 of the thesis (MARINHO, A.S., UFSCar 2015 — the repo’s benchmark.json); competing heuristics from Thevenin, Zufferey & Widmer (2015). MaScLib instances run on the validated Rust engine compiled to WASM.
